Hearty Beef Stew

Description

This classic beef stew is the epitome of comfort food. Chunks of tender beef, carrots, and potatoes are simmered in a rich, flavorful broth until everything is fall-apart delicious. It's the perfect one-pot meal for a cold evening.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 3 potatoes, cubed
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Toss the beef cubes with flour, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches and set aside.
  3. Add the onion to the pot and cook until soft. Add garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Pour in the red wine (if using) and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Stir in beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, and bay leaf.
  5. Return the beef to the pot. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat, cover, and cook for 1.5 hours.
  6. Add the carrots and potatoes. Cook for another 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables and beef are tender.
  7. Remove the bay leaf before serving. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
